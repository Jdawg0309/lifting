How GZCL Programs Helped Me Achieve Impressive Progress in My Lifts

Are you struggling to make progress in your lifts despite your best efforts? If so, the GZCL programs might be just what you need to take your strength training to the next level.

I was in the same boat as you. I had been lifting for years and had hit a wall in terms of progress. I tried all sorts of training programs and strategies, but nothing seemed to work. That is, until I discovered the GZCL programs.

GZCL, or "The Generalized Intermediate Program," is a powerlifting program designed by Cody Lefever. It is a highly customizable program that is designed to help lifters of all levels reach their strength goals. The program is based on a simple formula: "GZCL," which stands for "The General, The Accumulation, The Intensification, and The Peaking."

The program is broken down into four phases, each of which is designed to help you build strength and break through plateaus. During The General phase, you focus on building general strength through a variety of exercises. The Accumulation phase focuses on volume training, helping you build muscle mass and endurance. The Intensification phase is where you really start to push yourself, using heavy weights and low reps to build maximal strength. Finally, during The Peaking phase, you work on fine-tuning your technique and preparing for competition.

What I love about the GZCL program is how customizable it is. You can choose which exercises to focus on, and how much volume and intensity to incorporate into each phase. This allows you to tailor the program to your individual needs and goals.

Personally, I saw incredible progress in my lifts while using the GZCL program. My squat, bench, and front squat numbers all skyrocketed, and I was able to break through plateaus that I had been stuck at for years. For example, I was squatting 345 pounds before starting the program, and now I can squat 340 pounds for four reps at an RPE 8, which is an incredible improvement.

But the GZCL program isn't just about getting stronger. It's also about addressing weaknesses in your lifts and improving your overall form. For example, I had a quad weakness that was causing me to lean forward during my low bar squats. By incorporating more front squats into my training, I was able to strengthen my quads and improve my squat form.

If you're looking to take your strength training to the next level and break through plateaus, I highly recommend the GZCL program. With its customizable approach and focus on addressing weaknesses, it can help you achieve impressive progress in your lifts.
